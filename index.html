How to use this with your class

  1. Pick an image — portraits, animals, famous artworks, and line drawings all work great.
  2. Set the grid — larger grids (6×6, 8×8) are more challenging; 4×4 or 5×5 suits beginners.
  3. Download and print — the PNG includes both the scrambled tiles and the blank grid on one sheet.
  4. Students draw — each tile is labeled with its grid coordinate. Students find the matching square on the blank grid and recreate what they see.
  5. Tip: encourage students to look at each tile in isolation and draw only the shapes and lines they see — not what they think the image "should" look like.
